NOROVIRUS​

Norovirus Epidemic 2019​

Symptoms

  • Nausea​
  • Vomiting​
  • Abdominal pain or cramps​
  • diarrhea​
  • Malaise​
  • Low-grade fever​
  • Muscle pain​
  • Begins 24- 48 hours after exposure and can still be contagious 1-2 weeks after symptoms disappear ​

How do you get infected?​

  • Spread through:​
  •         - Food prepared on contaminated Surfaces ​
  •         - Eating or drinking liquids that are contaminated ​
  •         - direct contact with infected person​
  •         - touching mouth before washing hands​
  • Can be spread through eg. Undercooked Oysters from contaminated water ​

Treatment

  • Currently there are no medications or vaccines ​
  • Drink a lot of water to reduce dehydration​
  • Rest ​

The Chemistry​

  • Caliciviruses, are category B priority pathogens. ​
  • Are a pathogen threat as they are very contagious ​
  • And they are highly stable in the environment​
  • How could contagious diseases like the Norovirus affect communities that are less fortunate (do not have access to health services)?​
  • If we achieve Goal 3, how could that change?​

What can the School do?​

  • Enforce hygienic standards​
  • Use special disinfection​
  • Infected students not allowed on school campus​
